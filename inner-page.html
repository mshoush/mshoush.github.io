Research Statement

My research interest is centered on the development and application of advanced Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning techniques to address complex real-life challenges, with a strong emphasis on socio-technical implications, trustworthiness, and real-world impact. My work lies at the confluence of Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence, Information Systems, and Trustworthy Digital Systems, seeking to create intelligent, actionable, and ethically sound AI solutions.

Research Vision

My overarching vision is to pioneer AI methodologies that not only predict and optimize but also prescribe and govern actions within dynamic, human-centric systems, especially in areas of science, medicine, and broader societal benefit. This involves moving beyond purely technical AI development to explicitly consider the integration of AI into complex operational environments, acknowledging uncertainty, resource constraints, human factors, and ethical imperatives. I aim to foster trust in AI systems by ensuring their transparency, fairness, and privacy-preservation.

Key Research Areas

  • Trustworthy and Privacy-Preserving AI (Generative AI & Synthetic Data): Developing high-fidelity synthetic data generation models that preserve privacy, ensure fairness, and maintain utility for downstream tasks. This is crucial for data democratization in sensitive domains like healthcare and finance.
  • Prescriptive Process Monitoring and Reinforcement Learning: Designing RL frameworks to dynamically recommend interventions in business processes, explicitly accounting for uncertainty and resource constraints to improve operational KPIs.
  • Causal Inference and Explainable AI (XAI) for Decision Support: Leveraging causal inference to understand intervention impacts and using XAI methods (e.g., Shapley values) to make complex models transparent and interpretable.
  • Socio-technical Aspects of AI Deployment: Exploring how AI impacts human decision-making, organizational roles, and social norms, aiming to design socially intelligent and adaptable AI systems.

Research Methodology

I adopt a multi-faceted approach integrating: Design Science Research (iterative artifact development), Quantitative and Qualitative Analysis, Computational Modeling (RL, generative models, probabilistic modeling), and Empirical Validation using real-world event logs, synthetic datasets, and industrial case studies.

Future Research Directions

I aim to explore Human-AI Collaboration in Autonomous Systems, AI for Biomedical and Health Applications (e.g., prescriptive AI for diagnostics and treatment planning), Ethical AI and Algorithmic Governance (fairness, accountability, transparency), and Scalable and Distributed AI Infrastructure.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy focuses on bridging the gap between theoretical concepts and practical application at the intersection of business data and AI. I view teaching as a collaborative process where students learn by doing, applying theory to realistic, data-driven problems to become proficient problem-solvers. I encourage an inclusive, hands-on learning environment that emphasizes analytical reasoning, critical thinking, and reproducible research practices.

Core Principles

  • Theory-to-Practice Integration: Courses integrate real-world case studies, industry-relevant datasets, and project-based learning.
  • Active Learning and Modern Pedagogies: Interactive lectures, collaborative projects, coding assignments, simulations, and discussions.
  • Mentorship and Skill Development: Guiding students in analytical reasoning, critical evaluation of AI models, reproducible research, and effective communication.
  • Fostering a Growth Mindset: Encouraging students to embrace challenges, learn from mistakes, and cultivate curiosity.
  • Inclusivity and Diversity: Creating a supportive environment where all students feel empowered to succeed.

Courses Taught

  • Business Data Analytics (Master's): Full responsibility for course design, lecture delivery, and assessment. Topics include CRISP-DM, A/B testing, causal inference, Python/R for business problem-solving.
  • Python for Data Science (Crash Course): Intensive course for 50+ bachelor's and master's students without prior programming experience.
  • Communicating Science (PhD Program): Helping PhD students improve presentation and research communication skills.

Supervision

I have supervised numerous bachelor's and master's students on industry-collaborative projects, resulting in publications and a Best Demo Paper Award. I am committed to mentoring students through independent research and guiding them towards impactful outcomes.

Contributions to Future Institutions

I am keen to teach and develop curriculum in Machine Learning & AI, Data Science, Trustworthy AI & Ethics, Business Process Management, and Programming for Data Science. I embrace research-led teaching, integrating cutting-edge research to provide students with exposure to latest advancements.
